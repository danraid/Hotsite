# Design Quality Checklist

## Inputs

- Required input files were searched in the defined order.
- An input-status table records missing, partial, conflicting, and unverified sources.
- No missing business fact was silently invented.
- Public credentials and professional claims are marked for verification when needed.

## Product and conversion

- The primary conversion is explicit.
- CTA destinations are known or marked `[OPEN]`.
- Every section has a conversion or comprehension purpose.
- The narrative supports the audience's likely awareness level.

## Content and trust

- Claims require identified evidence or approval.
- Copy and asset ownership are explicit.
- Health-related language does not imply guaranteed outcomes or replacement of indicated care.
- Missing legal, contact, and footer content is identified.

## UX and visual system

- Mobile behavior is described, not implied.
- Long-form content has a readable hierarchy.
- Color combinations are checked for contrast.
- Font licensing and fallbacks are addressed.
- Motion is restrained and respects reduced-motion preferences.

## Technical and operational

- Forms include validation, success, error, privacy, and spam handling.
- SEO, analytics, accessibility, privacy, security, and performance are first-class requirements.
- Technical choices are justified against repository constraints.
- No unresolved critical question is hidden.
- The recommended spec boundaries are independently implementable.

## Status

- Requirements have stable IDs and sources.
- Material decisions are recorded.
- Approval blockers are visible.
- The handoff status follows `status-and-handoff.md`.

# Brand Guidelines — Janaína Hollanda

## Brand concept

> Silêncio, elegância e profundidade.

The visual identity should communicate confidence rather than demand attention. The experience should feel quiet, refined, warm, natural, and emotionally safe.

## Brand attributes

- Stability
- Maturity
- Welcome
- Nature
- Growth
- Serenity
- Depth
- Trust
- Discretion
- Premium restraint

| Token | Name | HEX | Intended use |
|---|---|---:|---|
| `color-accent-petrol` | Azul Petróleo Profundo | `#4B6A72` | Rare, deliberate detail such as one special action, icon, or illustration |

Use the petrol blue sparingly. It must not compete with the olive primary color.

## Color rules

- Use olive as the dominant brand/action color.
- Use off-white as the default page canvas.
- Use linen beige to create warmth and separate content groups.
- Never use matte gold as the dominant background or main text color.
- Validate all text/background combinations against WCAG AA.
- Do not use `#B9B3A7` for small body copy unless contrast is sufficient.
- Define hover, active, focus, disabled, and visited states from the approved palette rather than introducing unrelated colors.

## Typography

### Headings

Preferred:

1. Cormorant Garamond
2. Canela, only when a valid web license is available

### Body

Preferred options:

1. Avenir, only when a valid web license is available
2. Lato
3. Source Sans 3

### Typography rules

- Do not assume licensed fonts are available.
- Prefer a legally usable, performant fallback when licensing is unconfirmed.
- Use serif typography for depth and editorial character in headings.
- Use a highly readable sans-serif for body copy, controls, labels, and utility text.
- Avoid excessively light font weights.
- Maintain comfortable line length and line height for long-form emotional content.

## Photography direction

Prefer images featuring:

- Natural window light
- Olive branches and leaves
- Stones
- Natural textiles
- Handmade ceramics
- Paths
- Water
- Books
- Coffee
- Hands
- Nature
- Bright, quiet interiors

## Photography restrictions

Avoid:

- Highly produced commercial stock imagery
- Exaggerated smiles
- Subjects posing directly at the camera
- Clinical or hospital-like environments
- Overly dark, dramatic, or mystical imagery
- Visual clichés that trivialize emotional distress
- Images implying guaranteed transformation

## Composition and art direction

- Use natural textures and soft light.
- Favor negative space and restrained compositions.
- Keep image treatment warm and low-contrast.
- Avoid visual clutter and decorative excess.
- Use subtle lines, botanical details, and tactile materials.

## Motion direction

- Motion must be slow, subtle, and functional.
- Avoid parallax-heavy, bouncing, flashing, or attention-seeking effects.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## Voice principles

- Warm but not sentimental
- Deep but understandable
- Professional but not cold
- Inviting but not coercive
- Reflective but not vague
- Respectful of personal autonomy

## Prohibited communication patterns

- Guaranteed outcomes
- Fear-based urgency
- Diagnostic claims without proper context
- Claims of cure
- Superiority claims without evidence
- Language that blames visitors for their suffering
- Excessive spiritualization of health conditions

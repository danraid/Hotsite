# Project Brief — Janaína Hollanda Hotsite

## Document status

- Status: Draft
- Source: Client-provided briefing and homepage copy
- Primary language: Portuguese (Brazil)
- Intended artifact: Conversion-focused professional hotsite

## Project summary

Create a calm, premium, trustworthy hotsite for Janaína Hollanda's therapeutic practice. The site should communicate discretion, depth, emotional safety, and professional credibility without using aggressive sales language or visually overstimulating patterns.

## Primary objective

Encourage qualified visitors to initiate a private first conversation about therapeutic support.

## Primary conversion

- CTA label: `Agendar uma conversa`
- Destination: `[OPEN: scheduling URL, form, WhatsApp flow, or calendar tool]`

## Secondary conversions

- `Conhecer os atendimentos`
- `Conhecer minha abordagem`
- `Conhecer minha trajetória`
- `Conhecer o atendimento individual`
- `Conhecer a Constelação Familiar`
- `Conhecer as próximas vivências`
- `Entrar em contato pelo WhatsApp`

## Intended audience

Adults seeking emotional care, self-knowledge, personal development, or support with anxiety, sadness, stress, overload, relationship patterns, transitions, grief, boundaries, self-criticism, and meaning.

Detailed audience segmentation remains `[OPEN]`.

## Core value proposition

Offer a reserved, respectful, individualized therapeutic space that integrates multiple perspectives to help each person understand emotions, recurring patterns, personal history, relationships, values, choices, and meaning.

## Brand promise

A space of welcome, discretion, depth, and conscious personal development.

## Brand concept

> Silêncio, elegância e profundidade.

The site should not seek attention through excess. It should build confidence through clarity, restraint, warmth, and consistency.

## Success metrics

- Primary CTA click-through rate
- Qualified conversation starts
- WhatsApp contact starts

- Mobile conversion rate

Targets and analytics tools are `[OPEN]`.

## Constraints

- Avoid clinical, cold, or hospital-like aesthetics.
- Avoid exaggerated claims, guarantees, and urgency.
- Avoid highly produced stock photography.
- Avoid exaggerated smiling portraits looking directly at the camera.
- Preserve a calm reading rhythm and generous whitespace.
- Do not imply that therapeutic care replaces indicated medical, psychological, psychiatric, or other health care.
